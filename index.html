A Kansas Hero's Story: Remembering Harold D. Dolezal

Harold D. Dolezal embodied the greatest virtues of his generation—courage, dedication, and unwavering commitment to his family and country. Born in 1915 in Belleville, Kansas, Harold answered his nation's call during World War II, serving honorably in the United States Army from 1941 to 1945.

His military service took him across multiple theaters of war, from the shores of North Africa to the heart of Europe, demonstrating remarkable bravery through campaigns in Southern France, Germany, Italy, Tunisia, and beyond.

In every battle, there is a hero who faces the storm with unwavering courage. Your bravery will never fade, and your legacy will never be forgotten. -Unknown

Just months after enlisting, Harold married the love of his life, Velma Hamil, beginning a beautiful family journey that blessed them with three children: Dale, Cheryl, and Judy. Upon returning home, Harold channeled his steadfast work ethic into farming across Republic and Washington County for many years, cultivating not just the land but also deep roots in his community through his membership in the American Legion and VFW.

"The greatest legacy one can pass on to one's children and grandchildren is not money or other material things accumulated in one's life, but rather a legacy of character and faith." - Billy Graham

Though he departed this world in 2012 at the age of 97, Harold's legacy lives on through his children, nine grandchildren, sixteen great-grandchildren, and the countless lives he touched with his quiet strength and generous spirit.
